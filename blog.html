Latent Agent Communication: Eliminating Redundant Prefill in Multi-Agent LLM Pipelines

Every agent handoff in a same-model pipeline re-computes what the previous agent already did. 6 experiments. 325 timed runs. 6.74× prefill speedup. Here's what we found...

1/5

The hidden tax in multi-agent pipelines. When Agent A finishes and hands off to Agent B, standard practice decodes A's output to text — then Agent B re-tokenizes and re-prefills the entire sequence before generating its first token.

That computation is identical to what Agent A already did. In a 4-hop pipeline, you pay for 3 full wasted prefills. At production scale this is not a rounding error.

2/5

The fix is conceptually simple: pass the KV cache directly. Instead of decoding Agent A's output to text and re-prefilling, extract the KV slice corresponding to A's generation tokens and hand it to Agent B. Agent B only prefills its own system prompt (~41 tokens).

The primitive required from a provider is minimal: a session_id field on inference calls — enough to route chained calls to the same GPU process. One critical detail: RoPE positional encodings must be re-indexed or generation immediately fails.

3/5

The numbers, measured on Jetson AGX Orin with Qwen2.5 models:

  • 🔹 EXP-001: 3.22× speedup on 3B model, 20 runs, CV <1% — baseline confirmed
  • 🔹 EXP-002: Without RoPE re-indexing, generation immediately breaks — non-negotiable
  • 🔹 EXP-003: Savings scale with context length — 1.0× at 50 tokens, up to 66× at 4,000 tokens
  • 🔹 EXP-004: 6.74× on the 7B model — speedup grows with model size as expected
4/5

Transfer cost is the key engineering question. In-process KV cache clone: 1.4 ms — just 0.2% of the 581 ms prefill saving. Cross-process via shared memory: 73 ms, still only 12.6% of the saving.

Real providers using CUDA IPC (same GPU, different process) would pay ~14 µs — under 0.01%. Quality held: 30/30 tests across 5 cross-process runs.

5/5

The provider argument. A 4-hop pipeline saves ~1.7 seconds of prefill per call (3 hops × ~580 ms/hop). At 10M calls/day, that's roughly 4,850 GPU-hours/day of avoidable compute. The 6.74× speedup ratio is the hardware-agnostic result — it scales with model size and context length, not with Jetson's absolute timing.

Rule of thumb: build this when prior_context_tokens / system_prompt_tokens > 3. Most real agent pipelines naturally exceed this ratio. Read the full report →

Carrick, AFCON, and the Question of Genuine Progress at Manchester United

Carrick's United have more points than Amorim's. The xG data tells a much more complicated story about what's actually happening at Old Trafford...

1/4

Three events collided at the same moment. Amorim sacked on 5 January. AFCON 2025 running 21 Dec – 18 Jan, taking Amad Diallo and Bryan Mbeumo out for five Premier League matches. Carrick appointed 13 January — inheriting a squad about to get its two best players back.

The question the data needs to answer: is Carrick's improvement real, or were United always going to look better once the AFCON players returned — whoever was in the dugout?

2/4

The points picture looks compelling — until you look at xPts.

  • 🔹 Amorim: 31 actual pts from 20 games — almost exactly matching his 32.9 xPts deserved
  • 🔹 Carrick: 26 actual pts from 12 games — massively over-performing his 18.6 xPts

That +7.4 point gap over 12 matches is an outlier. Amorim was getting what his performances merited. Carrick is getting far more than his.

3/4

AFCON did hurt Amorim — and the magnitude is significant. Looking at the four AFCON-depleted matches in isolation: xPts/game dropped from 1.71 (Amorim with full squad) to 1.39 (Amorim without Amad/Mbeumo). Carrick's full-squad xPts/game is 1.55 — lower than Amorim's full-squad number.

On underlying performance metrics, Amorim's United with a full squad was outperforming Carrick's United.

4/4

The confound cannot be resolved — and that's the honest conclusion. Carrick took charge on 13 January. Amad and Mbeumo returned on 17 January. There is exactly one Carrick match without them and zero Amorim matches after their return.
